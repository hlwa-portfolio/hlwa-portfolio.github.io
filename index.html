High Level Waste Glass Application

A High-Level Waste Glass Vitrification Calculator made in collaboration with the Waste Treatment Completion Company and Oregon State University.

01 — Project Description

What Is It & Why Does It Matter?

The High Level Waste Glass Application Project is a software tool developed in collaboration with the Waste Treatment Completion Company and Oregon State University to support the vitrification of nuclear waste at the Hanford Site in Washington State. At its core, the project transforms a highly technical waste-glass formulation algorithm, made up of hundreds of chemical equations, into a usable application that guides Hanford Site operators through the calculation process.

Hanford has long faced major challenges in nuclear waste storage and cleanup. Decades of nuclear production left the site with large volumes of hazardous radioactive waste, creating one of the most complex environmental cleanup efforts in the United States. Some of that waste poses a long-term risk to the surrounding environment, including the Columbia River. The broader goal is to stabilize this waste through vitrification, turning it into glass so it can be stored and disposed of more safely.

This application is designed to directly support that mission. It implements the vitrification calculation workflow used to evaluate waste composition, calculate glass and oxide values, validate process constraints, automate reports, and support engineering decisions throughout the formulation process. Our tool is not only dramatically faster, but also more repeatable, scalable, and practical for the engineers carrying out this work at the site.

02 — Key Features & Highlights

What Can It Do?

The calculator handles the core algorithmic steps in the Immobilized High-Level Waste product compliance workflow, turning lab sample data into the engineering values needed for vitrification. Unifying these steps creates a seamless production tool for the vitrification operators at the Hanford site.

✦

Step 1: Waste Transfer

Waste samples are used to calculate the required High-Level Waste (HLW) transfer into Melter Feed Preparation Vessels (MFPV), establishing the starting point for the batch.

✦

Step 2: GFC Calculation

After the waste is transferred, new samples are analyzed to calculate the required glass-forming chemical (GFC) masses needed to adjust the mixture.

✦

Step 3: Glass Property Prediction

After the GFCs are added, the final mixture is sampled and analyzed to predict the glass properties, giving engineers a data-driven estimate of whether the glass will meet requirements.

✦

Step 4: Constraint Validation

The predicted properties and composition are validated against required process and product constraints. If the formulation does not pass, the workflow loops back to Step 2 to adjust the mixture.

✦

Step 5: Record Generation

Production records are generated and saved, supporting production record generation and process readiness for the vitrification operators.
