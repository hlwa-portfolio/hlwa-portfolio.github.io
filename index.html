High Level Waste Glass Application

A High-Level Waste Glass Vitrification Calculator made in collaboration with the Waste Treatment Completion Company and Oregon State University.

01 — What It Is & Why It Matters

Nuclear Waste Vitrification

The process of converting radioactive waste slurry into glass forms

Background
  • Hanford was the site of Plutonium enrichment for the Manhattan Project. They produced the Plutonium used in the Fat Man bomb dropped on Nagasaki. Today, the site has thousands of tanks of radioactive waste slurry.
  • Many of the tanks are old, leaking, or about to leak.
  • In order to protect the Columbia River, Hanford is using vitrification to contain nuclear waste as glass to prevent further contamination.
What Is Vitrification?
  • The process of adding glass forming chemicals (GFCs) to the radioactive waste slurry and heating the mixture so they incorporate into a viscous fluid.
  • The resulting product is moved to canisters and cools into a stable glass-like structure.
  • The site began vitrification of low activity waste on October 15th, 2025.
What We Solve
  • We created an application that simplifies the 300+ equations required for the process into a calculator & optimizer application that only takes minutes for each step.
  • Previous implementations of the Algorithm would take weeks for operators to apply.
  • Now operators can easily execute the algorithm for each step of the vitrification process and achieve optimal glass compositions.
  • In addition to operations, our app also automatically creates and saves reports for each batch.
02 — Calculator Features

High Level Waste Glass Vitrification Algorithm Explained

The calculator handles the core algorithmic steps in the Immobilized High-Level Waste product compliance workflow, turning lab sample data into the engineering values needed for vitrification. Unifying these steps creates a seamless production tool for the vitrification operators at the Waste Treatment Completion Company at the Hanford site.

Step 1 Waste Volume Transfer
→

Step 1: Waste Volume Transfer

Waste samples are used to calculate the required High-Level (HLW) transfer into Melter Feed Preparation Vessel.

Step 2 GFC/Glass Transfer
→

Step 2: GFC Calculation

After the waste is transferred, new samples are analyzed to calculate the required glass-forming chemical (GFC) masses needed.

Step 3 Predict Properties
→

Step 3: Glass Property Prediction

After the GFCs are added, the final mixture is sampled and analyzed to predict the glass properties.

Step 4 Validate Properties
→

Step 4: Constraint Validation

The predicted glass properties are validated against the requirements. If the formulation does not meet constraints, the workflow loops back to Step 2 to fix the mixture.

Step 5 Generate Report
→

Step 5: Record Generation

The production records are generated and saved.
